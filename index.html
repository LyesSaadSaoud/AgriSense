Project overview

From greenhouse observation
to validated robotic action.

AgriSense is an embodied autonomy framework for greenhouse inspection under GNSS-denied conditions. The robot acquires RGB, LiDAR, IMU, and environmental observations while navigating a structured digital-twin greenhouse.

A vision-language decision-to-action module generates structured navigation, environmental-control, and intervention proposals. These proposals are validated for confidence, freshness, feasibility, localization consistency, and manipulator reachability before execution.

System architecture
01

High-fidelity greenhouse digital twin

A structured Isaac Sim environment containing greenhouse geometry, cultivation beds, crop rows, inspection corridors, robot-view sensing, and environmental context.

02

Vision-language decision-to-action

User instructions, RGB observations, greenhouse sensor states, and robot spatial context are fused to produce structured task, zone, setpoint, and intervention proposals.

03

GNSS-denied spatial grounding

LiDAR and IMU observations support local odometry, map construction, zone association, and physically consistent robot localization without GNSS.

04

Plant-level robotic intervention

Zone-level decisions are refined into plant-level inspection and targeted manipulator actions after safety and reachability validation.

Spatial awareness

Semantic decisions remain tied to physical space.

Robot observations are associated with a local pose, map, and greenhouse zone. This spatial grounding allows the autonomy stack to connect a semantic instruction to a reproducible physical inspection or intervention target.

Software and reproducibility

Code, training, evaluation,
and dataset organization.

The repository is organized to expose the complete research workflow rather than only the project webpage. It includes model components, training and evaluation scripts, configuration files, digital-twin integration resources, and reproducibility documentation.

The associated AgriSense dataset is currently maintained in a private Hugging Face repository during peer review. The public dataset link will be added following acceptance.
